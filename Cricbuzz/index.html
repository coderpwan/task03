Daniel Worrall | Player of the Match: The first one did not come out beautifully but when you get a lucky start like that, it is easy to come back. That's what I said to Marco, when I got one down the leg-side, they all count. Just being a swing bowler and trying to extract as much as I can from the pitch, that's the role I have in the team. Being an old guy, I need to adapt to different situations. Hoping to carry that to the finals. It's a fine line, the conditions dictate how full you can be and how greedy you can be. Aiden Markram | SEC captain: That was a nice performance. Few balls to get in the right area, quite fortunate with the other dismissals. Faf du Plessis | JSK captain: A little bit shocked to be honest with you. Not batting well and not assessing the conditions. It was not pitch you get 170, also one of those days when everything went wrong. Three burgles down the leg-side. Momentum is against you and we have to fight to change that. It feels like one of those days. You sit there and think, surely not, not another one down leg. That's how cricket has worked like that for a long time, when you are batting well, you get dropped chances. Disappointing after Cape Town and especially with the tournament going the way, we have made the last game tough for ourselves. It is like a blooper, you have to try and bury it quickly. You got to look ahead, one game left against a Durban side who are playing very well. But if we play well we are a dangerous side. You don't want to go into the powerplay three down, it breaks the back and that's the learning for the next game.
